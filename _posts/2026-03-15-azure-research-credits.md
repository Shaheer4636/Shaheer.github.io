I am glad to share that I have been awarded **$5,000 in Microsoft Azure for Research credits** through Microsoft's research program.

These credits will directly support my ongoing work in cloud infrastructure and Kubernetes-based ML platform development — areas I have been building expertise in since my internship at the National Centre for Physics (NCP) and my Final Year Project at PIEAS.

## What This Enables

With this Azure funding, I plan to:

- **Extend the MLFaaS research platform** by replicating the on-premise Kubernetes/Kubeflow setup in a cloud-native Azure environment (AKS), enabling direct comparison of resource scheduling behaviour across bare-metal and managed clusters.
- **Benchmark hybrid orchestration** — studying how workloads behave when scheduled across on-premises and cloud nodes, which is central to my PhD research goal of building orchestration layers that unify on-premises and cloud clusters.
- **Run experiments for the paper currently under review** — "Kubernetes-Powered Machine Learning Facility-as-a-Service: Design, Deployment, and Evaluation" — using Azure to validate results at scale beyond what on-premise infrastructure allows.

## Context

My research interest sits at the intersection of distributed systems and cloud infrastructure. Every large-scale computation — from a simple deployment to training an LLM — depends on clusters, schedulers, and resource managers working in sync. Azure's research credits give me the environment to study these dynamics without the constraints of a fixed on-premise cluster.

This is an exciting step forward. I look forward to sharing findings here as the work progresses.
