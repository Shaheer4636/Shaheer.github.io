Multi-agent AI systems — where autonomous agents interact, delegate, and share context across boundaries — introduce a class of security and privacy challenges structurally different from those in traditional software systems. This research investigates:

- How **sensitive data propagates** across agent handoffs and trust boundaries
- What **leakage vectors** emerge in orchestration and coordination layers
- How **distributed system design principles** can inform containment strategies for agentic deployments

The work draws on formal threat modeling, empirical analysis of agent communication patterns, and evaluation of architectural controls including sandboxing, access scoping, and audit mechanisms.
